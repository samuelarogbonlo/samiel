## About Me

<img class="profile-picture" src="{{ '/assets/images/profile.svg' | relative_url }}" alt="Samuel Arogbonlo">

I’m Samuel Arogbonlo. I build software for healthcare verification, patient access, and institutional trust.

Currently, I’m working on Pisgah, a system for making clinical workflows easier to verify across hospitals, patients, pharmacies, and field teams. The work sits at the intersection of healthcare operations, digital identity, device verification, and evidence trails.

I like simple systems, direct writing, and software that survives contact with messy real-world institutions.

## Projects

* **Pisgah.** Verification infrastructure for healthcare workflows. Pisgah is focused on proving who did what, when, and under which institution, without turning operations into paperwork for its own sake.

* **Prescription redemption verification.** A workflow for checking that a prescription redemption event is tied to the right patient, facility, and actor.

* **Device verification.** Work on field-device and operational-device checks for health programs where trust depends on evidence captured outside the main office.

* **Public notes.** Short essays and technical logs about building software for constrained, high-trust environments.
